Problem 1

Problem 1.1

The decision variables are:

  • $x_g$, which is the installed capacity (in MW) for generator type $g$;

  • $y_{g,h}$, which is the amount of electricity (in MWh) generated in hour $h$ by generator type $g$.

  • $nse_h$, which is the amount of non-served demand (in MWh) in hour $h$.

Problem 1.2

The cost function $Z(x_g, y_{g, h}, nse_h)$ is the sum of the installed cost $Z^I(x_g)$, the operational cost $Z^O(y_{g,h})$, and the cost of non-served energy $Z^{NSE}(nse_h)$. Denote by $IC_g$ the installed cost of generator type $g$ in $/MW-yr, $OC_g$ the operational cost of generator $g$ in $/MWh, and $NC$ the cost of non-served energy in $/MWh.

\[ \begin{align*} Z &= Z^I(x_g) + Z^O(y_{g,h}) + Z^{NSE}(nse_h) \\ &= \sum_g IC_g \times x_g + \sum_g \sum_h OC_g y_{g,h} + \sum_h NC \times nse_h \\ &= 457000 x_\text{geo} + 268000 x_\text{coal} + 85000 x_\text{ccgt} + 62580 x_\text{ct} + 92000 x_\text{wind} + 92000 x_\text{solar} \\ & \qquad + 365 \times \sum_{h=1}^{24} \left(22 y_{\text{coal}, h} + 35 y_{\text{ccgt}, h} + 45 y_{\text{ct}, h} + 1000 nse_h\right). \end{align*} \]

Problem 1.3

Our first set of constraints is that generated electricity plus non-served electricity must equal demand. Denoting by $d_h$ the demand in hour `h (in MWh):

\[ \sum_g y_{g,h} + nse_h = d_h. \]

Next, generation from any plant type in any hour is limited by available capacity. Denoting by $CF_{g,h}$ the capacity factor of plant type $g$ in hour $h$:

\[ y_{g,h} \leq CF_{g,h} \times x_g. \]

Finally, we have non-negativity constraints on all of our decision-variables:

\[ \begin{align*} x_g &\geq 0 \\ y_{g,h} &\geq 0 \\ nse_h &\geq 0 \end{align*} \]
